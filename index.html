My Story

I didn’t get into cyber security because I love machines. I got into it because I saw how easily normal people — parents, students, small business owners — were being left behind by a digital world that keeps changing faster than their confidence.

In my work as a lecturer and PhD researcher, I kept hearing the same thing: “I feel exposed, but I don’t even know what I should be worried about.”

This lab is my answer to that: a calm space where we translate digital risk, AI, UX and safety into everyday language — and practical actions you can actually take.

AI Is Not Just a Buzzword — It Changes the Risk Map

AI can write emails, mimic voices, generate fake images and scan huge amounts of data in seconds. That’s powerful — and it works for everyone, including scammers and attackers.

  • AI makes phishing emails look more realistic and personalised.
  • Deepfakes can copy faces and voices to trick people into sending money or data.
  • Large language models can analyse public information about you in seconds.
  • Most people’s data is scattered across dozens of apps, forms and platforms.

The solution isn’t to avoid AI; it’s to strengthen the human side: digital hygiene, identity awareness, good UX and clear boundaries.

Think of this as your digital health check space — where AI is acknowledged, but you stay in charge.
