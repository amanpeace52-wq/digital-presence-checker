UX basics checklist

A simple yes/no checklist to tidy up your website experience and reduce friction for visitors.

  • The main headline clearly says who you help and how.
  • There is one primary call to action above the fold.
  • Navigation links are short, clear and not overloaded.
  • Text is broken into short paragraphs or bullet points.
  • Buttons look like buttons and are easy to tap on mobile.
  • There is an obvious way to contact you or ask a question.
  • At least one trust signal is visible (testimonial, logo, case study).
  • Pages load reasonably quickly and images are not huge.
  • Important content is readable on mobile without zooming.
0 of 9 items ticked.

Use this when…

  • You’ve just launched or refreshed a website.
  • You want to make your site feel a little kinder and clearer.
  • You are preparing for a bigger redesign and want quick wins first.

You don’t need to tick everything at once. Even moving from 2/9 to 4/9 is a real improvement.
